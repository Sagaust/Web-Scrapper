Tahar Bekri

Biography

Tahar Bekri was born in 1951 in Gabès, Tunisia. A poet and literary critic, he has published several poetry collections, including La nostalgie des rosiers sauvages (2014) and Mûrier triste dans le printemps arabe (2016). Important themes in his work include exile, wandering, discovery of new worlds, and the reinvention of cultures. In 1970, he obtained his BA in Modern Literature from the University of Tunis. Considered one of the leading voices of contemporary North African literature, Bekri’s work has been the subject of academic research and translated into several languages (Russian, English, Italian, Spanish, Turkish, etc). In 1993, he was awarded the Order of Cultural Merit by the Tunisian Republic and in 2018, became the laureate of the Benjamin Fondane International Prize for Francophone Literature.
