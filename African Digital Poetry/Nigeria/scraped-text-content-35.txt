Ben Okri

Biography

Ben Okri was born in 1959 in Minna, Nigeria. A novelist, playwright, and a poet, Okri is the author of the 1992 poetry collection An African Elegy. His other collections include Wild (2012) and A Fire in My Head: Poems for the Dawn (2021). Okri attended Essex University in London where he studied Comparative Literature. His work, noted for its spiritual and magical realism, has received several awards and honors. He has been a Fellow Commoner in Creative Arts at Trinity College, Cambridge, and is a vice-president of the English Centre of International PEN. In 2012, Okri was appointed the new vice-president of the Caine Prize for African Writing and he’s currently on the board of the Booker Prize Foundation.
