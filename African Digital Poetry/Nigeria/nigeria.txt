Inua Ellams

Biography

Inua Ellams was born 1984 in Jos, Nigeria and moved to England with his family when he was 12. Playwright, performer, and poet, he explores themes of identity, displacement, and destiny in his work. He is also known to incorporate hip pop rhymes in his writings and experiment with the blend of traditional and contemporary styles. He is a member of the Royal Society of Literature and has received several awards including the 2020 Hay Medal for Poetry, the 2019 Magma Poetry Prize, and the 2018 Winchester Poetry Prize. He first full-length poetry collection, The Actual (2020), was longlisted for the Derek Walcott Poetry Prize. Ellams studied at Firhouse Community College in Dublin, Ireland from 1999-2002 and in 2006, he attended a Poetry in Education Training programme by Apples and Snakes, UK’s leading spoken word organization. He lives in London.

Niran Okewole

Biography

Niran Okewole was born in 1977. He is a psychiatrist, playwright, essayist, and poet. He won the MUSON Festival Poetry Prize in 2002 and 2003 and was a recipient of the Sawubona Music Jam/Berlin International Poetry Festival Prize in 2008. Okewole’s poems have appeared in ANA Review, Mindfire Review, Farafina Magazine, African Writing, Saraba, and Maple Tree Literary Supplement. He has published two poetry collections: Logarhythms (2005) and The Hate Artist (2015).

Ifi Amadiume

Biography

Ifi Amadiume was Born in 1947 in Kaduna, Nigeria. A scholar, anthropologist and poet, Amadiume is the author of several collections of poetry including Passion Waves (London: Karnak House, 1985), Ecstasy (Longman Nigeria, 1995), Passion Circles of Love (Africa World Press, 2006) and Voices Draped in Black (Africa World Press 2007).  She is also the author of several books of scholarship including Male Daughters, Female Husbands: Gender and Sex in an African Society (1987) and Reinventing Africa: Matriarchy, Religion and Culture (1997, Zed Books). She studied at the University of London earning her BA in 1978 and her Phd in 1984. She was a professor of religion at Dartmouth College.

Tade Ipadeola

Biography

Ipadeola Tade was born in 1970 in Fiditi, Oyo State. A lawyer and a poet, he was awarded the 2013 NLNG Prize for his poetry collection titled The Sahara Testaments. Ipadeola has authored other poetry books, which include Voices: Interiors and Exteriors (1996), A Time of Signs (2000), and The Rain Fardel (2005). He attended Obafemi Awolowo University, where he graduated with a Law degree in 1991. Ipadeola writes in English and Yoruba, and he has translated two classical Yoruba novels by D.O. Fagunwa into English: The Divine Cryptograph (Aditu); and The Pleasant Potentate of Ibudo (Ireke Onibudo). He was selected for the Iowa Writing Fellowship in 2019.

Tanure Ojaide

Biography

Tanure Ojaide was born in Nigeria in 1948. He has a B.A. in English from University of Ibadan, Nigeria, and an M.A. (Creative Writing) and Ph.D. (English) from Syracuse University. An accomplished poet and academic, Ojaide is the author of many collections of poetry including Narrow Escape: A Poetic Diary of the Coronavirus Pandemic (2021), The Questioner (2018), Love Gifts (2013), The Beauty I Have Seen (2010), and Songs of Myself: A Quartet (2015), which won the 2018 Wole Soyinka Prize for Literature in Africa. He has won other awards for poetry including the Commonwealth Poetry Prize for the Africa Region (1987), the All-Africa Okigbo Prize for Poetry (1988, 1997), the BBC Arts and Africa Poetry Award (1988), and the Association of Nigerian Authors Poetry Award (1988, 1994, 2003, and 2011). He is currently the Frank Porter Graham Professor of Africana Studies at The University of North Carolina at Charlotte.

Pete Kalu

Biography

Pete Kalu was born in 1965 in Manchester, UK. He is the author of the 1996 poetry collection Mongrel Moon. Raised by a Danish mother and Nigerian father, Kalu explores cultural diversity in his work with a research focus on Postcolonial revisions of Imperial history, Black British crime fiction, and YA fiction. He attended Lancaster University for his PhD in Creative Writing and has a first degree in Law from Leeds University. A fiction writer and playwright, he has written several film scripts and radio and theatre plays, including No Trace, winner of the 2002 Kodak/ Liverpool Black Film Festival Award. Kalu was the elected Fellow for the Royal Society for the Arts in 2013 and was recognized among the Top 50 Black British Writers 2019: Speaking Volumes.

Niyi Osundare

Biography

Niyi Osundare was born 1947 in Ikere, Nigeria. Poet, playwright and essayist, he has published many poetry collections including Moonsongs (1988) and Random Blues (2011). He went to the University of Ibadan, Nigeria for his undergraduate studies. He also received his masters from the University of Leeds in England, and in 1979, he received his PhD from York University. Osundare has received several awards including the Commonwealth Poetry Prize (1989 and 1994), the Tchicaya U Tam'si Award for African Poetry, and the Fonlon/Nichols Award for his contributions to African literature and advocacy of freedom of expression. In 2005, he was selected as a Fellow of the Nigeria Academy of Letters (NAL). A Professor of English at the University of New Orleans, he specializes in African Literature, Literature of the African Diaspora, and Literary Stylistic.

Wole Soyinka

Alternate names: Akinwande Oluwole Babatunde

Biography

Wole Soyinka was born in 1934 in Abeokuta in western Nigeria. He has published six collections of poetry including Idanre, and Other Poems (1967), Poems from Prison (1969), A Shuttle in the Crypt (1972) the long poem Ogun Abibiman (1976) and Mandela’s Earth and Other Poems (1988). He attended the University College Ibadan in 1954 and studied English literature, Greek, and Western history. In 1957, he went on to the University of Leeds in England where he continued his studies in English literature. He is most well known for his work as a playwright in which he engages Yoruba folklore and indigenous spiritual traditions. He also dramatizes political and social issues in Nigeria. He was the first African to be awarded the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1986. Following his political advocacy, he went into exile during the military coup of January 1966. He was later imprisoned until his release in October 1969.

Patience Agbabi

Biography

Patience Agababi was born in 1965 in London, Britain to Nigerian parents, but was later raised in Wales by a foster family. She attended Pembroke college and earned an MA in Creative Writing from the University of Sussex in 2002. Afterwards, she became an associate Creative Writing Lecturer at the University of Wales, Cardiff. She is the author of several collections of poetry including, R.A.W. (1995), Transformatrix (2000), Bloodshot Monochrome (2008), Telling Tales (2014), and The Infinite (2020). In 1997, R.A.W received the Excelle Literary Award. In 2014, Telling Tales was shortlisted by the Poetry Society for the Ted Hughes Award. Agbabi also served as a fellow of the Royal Society of Literature in 2017.

Harry Garuba

Biography

Harry Garuba was born 1958 in Akure, Nigeria. At 24, he published his debut collection, Shadow and Dream and Other Poems (1982) which was first runner-up in the inaugural All-Africa Okigbo prize for poetry. In 1988, he received his MA and PhD from University of Ibadan, where he also served as an assistant lecturer in the English department from 1981 to 1997. Garuba was a member of the editorial advisory board of the Heinemann African Writers Series and was well known for his active interest in African and postcolonial literatures. In 1998, he moved to South Africa and joined the faculty at University of Cape Town in 2001. He died Cape Town at the age of 61.

Amatoritsero Ede

Amatoritsero Ede was born in Nigeria in 1963. Gabriel Okara

Biography

Gabriel Okara was born in Bumoundi (in present-day Bayelsa State), Nigeria in 1921. He studied art and writing at Yaba Higher College, and journalism at Northwestern University in Evanston, Illinois. In 1953 he won the Best All-Around award at the Nigerian Festival of Art for his poem “Call of the River Nun,” which would later be published in the literary magazine, Black Orpheus. His published poetry collections include The Dreamer, His Vision (2004), which won the 2005 NLNG Nigeria Prize for Literature, and The Fisherman’s Invocation (1978), for which he won the Commonwealth Joint Poetry Award in 1979. He died in 2019 in Yenagoa, Nigeria.

Ogaga Ifowodo

Biography

Ogaga Ifowodo was born May 14, 1966 in Nigeria. He has written four books of poetry— Homeland and Other Poems, Madiba,The Oil Lamp, and A Good Mourning. Ben Okri

Biography

Ben Okri was born in 1959 in Minna, Nigeria. A novelist, playwright, and a poet, Okri is the author of the 1992 poetry collection An African Elegy. His other collections include Wild (2012) and A Fire in My Head: Poems for the Dawn (2021). Okri attended Essex University in London where he studied Comparative Literature. His work, noted for its spiritual and magical realism, has received several awards and honors. He has been a Fellow Commoner in Creative Arts at Trinity College, Cambridge, and is a vice-president of the English Centre of International PEN. In 2012, Okri was appointed the new vice-president of the Caine Prize for African Writing and he’s currently on the board of the Booker Prize Foundation.

Nduka Otiono

Biography

Nduka Otiono was born 1964 in Kano, Nigeria. His poetry collection, Voice in the Rainbow (1997) was a finalist for the ANA/Cadbury Poetry Prize. The writer is known to employ ancestral oral tradition and experimental elements as he writes about the diasporic world. In 2019, he edited the anthology “Camouflage," which featured the best contemporary writing from Nigeria. Teacher and researcher, he obtained his BA and MA degrees from the University of Ibadan, Nigeria. He also received his PhD in English & Film Studies from the University of Alberta in 2011. Before relocating to Canada in 2006, he worked as a journalist in Nigeria and served as General Secretary of the Association of Nigerian Authors (ANA). He is a a two-time recipient of the Carnegie African Diaspora Fellowship (2015 and 2016). Otiono is an Assistant Professor at the Institute of African Studies, Carleton University, Ottawa, Canada.

Obi Nwakanma

Biography

Obi Nwakanma was born in 1966 in Ibadan, Oyo State. His first collection of poems, The Roped Urn, won the ANA / Cadbury Gabriel Okara Prize for Poetry in 2007. He also has two other poetry collections: The Horsemen And Other Poems (2007) and Birthcry (2016), which was longlisted for the 2013 Nigeria Prize for Literature. Poet, journalist and literary critic, his work focuses on a range of subjects including renaissance, cultural nationalism, decolonization, and representations within African literature. He earned a degree in English at the University of Jos and an MFA in Poetry from the Washington University in St. Louis. He also received his PhD in English from the Saint Louis University in St. Louis, Missouri. Nwakanma is a professor at the University of Central Florida where he teaches creative writing, transnational literatures, and black diaspora.

Olu Oguibe

Biography

Olu Oguibe was born in Aba, Nigeria, in 1964. An award-winning artist, academic, and writer, he has worked as a professor of art and African American studies and was a fellow at the Vera List Center for Art and Politics in 2000. He was a senior fellow of the Smithsonian Institution in 2006, and a resident scholar at the Rockefeller Study and Conference Center in Bellagio, Italy in 1999. He has published three books of poetry: I Am Bound to this Land by Blood (2013), A Song from Exile (1990), and A Gathering Fear, which won the 1992 Christopher Okigbo All-Africa Prize for Literature.
