Nduka Otiono

Biography

Nduka Otiono was born 1964 in Kano, Nigeria. His poetry collection, Voice in the Rainbow (1997) was a finalist for the ANA/Cadbury Poetry Prize. The writer is known to employ ancestral oral tradition and experimental elements as he writes about the diasporic world. In 2019, he edited the anthology “Camouflage," which featured the best contemporary writing from Nigeria. Teacher and researcher, he obtained his BA and MA degrees from the University of Ibadan, Nigeria. He also received his PhD in English & Film Studies from the University of Alberta in 2011. Before relocating to Canada in 2006, he worked as a journalist in Nigeria and served as General Secretary of the Association of Nigerian Authors (ANA). He is a a two-time recipient of the Carnegie African Diaspora Fellowship (2015 and 2016). Otiono is an Assistant Professor at the Institute of African Studies, Carleton University, Ottawa, Canada.
