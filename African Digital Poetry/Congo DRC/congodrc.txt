Juan Tomás   Ávila Laurel

Biography

Juan Tomás Ávila Laurel was born November 6th, 1966 in Equatorial Guinea. He has written several books of poetry, novels and short story collections in Spanish. He is most known for his novel By Night Mountains Burn, the second ever Equitoguniean book to be translated into English. His books of poetry include Historia Intima De La Humanidad (Intimate History of Humanity) among others. In 2011, he went on a much publicized hunger strike to protest the political dictatorship of Teodoro Obiang’s. Subsequently, he fled to Spain to escape persecution, where he continues to live, write and teach.

Lokangaka Losambe

Biography

Lokangaka Losambe was born in 1952 in Omutumba, Kasaï, Zaïre. Several of his poems have been published in Tyume Fort Hare Journal of Creative Writing. He attended National University of Zaire for his BA and MA in English language and literature. He also earned his M.Ed. from the University of Wales in 1979 and his PhD in African Literature from the University of Ibadan in 1983. Losambe’s research interests include African-Anglophone literature, Pan-African literature, Black Atlantic literature, and Post-colonialism. He is a Professor of English at the University of Vermont.

Juan Balboa Boneke

Biography

Juan Balboa Boneke was born in 1938 in Rebola, Equatorial Guinea. Writer and politician, he is the author of Sueños en mi selva: Antología poética (Dreams in My Jungle, 1987), O’Boriba (el exiliado) (O’Boriba [The Exiled], 1982), Requiebros (Flirtations, 1994), among others. He served as Minister under the presidency of Teodoro Obiang Nguema until he was forced to leave under the regimes of both Marcaís and Obiang. He went into exile in Palma de Mallorca and the theme of exile was central in his work. Boneke’s writing has also been regarded as political, with the writer advocating for the freedom to criticize dictatorship, corruption, and violence in his country with the hope to build a better nation. He obtained a Social degree from the University from Granada (Spain). In 2014, he died in Valencia, Spain.

Kama Sywor Kamanda

Biography

Kama Sywor Kamanda was born in 1952 in Congo. He has written over a dozen books of poetry, including Chants de brumes, Les Résignations, and L'Éternité des jours. He went to Journalism School Congo and University of Kinshasa for undergraduate degrees in Journalism and political science. He also received a Doctoral degree in Law from University Liège. He worked as a journalist and helped to establish the Union of Congolese Writers, but he was forced to leave Congo in 1977 because of his political activities. He eventually settled in Luxembourg.
