Patricia Jabbeh Wesley

Biography

Patricia Jabbeh Wesley was born 1955 in Monrovia, Liberia. She is the author is several poetry collections including Where the Road Turns (2010) and Praise song for my children: new and selected poems (2022). A Liberian civil war survivor, she explores themes of immigration, femininity, motherhood, displacement, war, and grief in her work. She incorporates African motifs and proverbs to capture the turmoil and recovery of her country’s struggle in war. She earned her BA at the University of Liberia and MS in English Education at Indiana University. She also pursued her PhD in Creative Writing at Western Michigan University. Wesley is the recipient of the 2010 Liberian Award for her poetry and her mentorship of young Liberians in the Diaspora. She is an Associate Professor of English, Creative Writing, and African Literature at Penn State University’s Altoona campus.
