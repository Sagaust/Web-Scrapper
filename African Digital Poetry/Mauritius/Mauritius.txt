Umar Timol

Biography

Umar Timol was born in 1970 in Mauritius. Poet, fiction writer and visual artist, he is the author of poetry collections La Parole Testament (2003), Sang (2004), and Vagabondages (2008). His other poetry collection, 52 Fragments pour l’aimée (52 Fragments for the Beloved) was the recipient of the 2016 Poetry Prize at the Moldova Poetry Festival. He received his BA in French Language and Literature from the University College London in 1993. Timol is currently at work on a project called ‘Muslim Faces : Breaking the Stereotypes’ where the writer engages in dialogue with Muslims to capture their stories as a way to resist stereotypes perpetuated in the media and create a platform to showcase the real and complex identities of Muslims.

Edouard Maunick

Biography

Édouard Maunick was born in 1931 in Flacq District, Mauritius. He is the author of several poetry collections including Les Oiseaux du Sang (1954), De Sable et de Cendre: Poèmes (1996) and Mascaret ou Le livre de la mer et de la mort, which was influenced by the Nigeria-Biafra war. Exile, captivity, history and identity are central themes in his work. After attending teacher's training college, he taught for nine years and worked as a librarian for the Municipality of Port Louis. At age twenty-nine, he moved from Port Louis to Europe in 1960 where he worked as writer and director for Coopération Radiophonique. Poet, critic and translator, he is the recipient of the 2003 Grand prix de la francophonie and the 1977 Apollinaire Prize for Poetry. He joined UNESCO in 1970 and served as the director of UNESCO collection of representative literary works and was a member of the Haut conseil de la Francophonie and a Chevalier des Arts et Lettres. Maunick was appointed as Mauritian ambassador to South Africa in 1994. He died in 2021 in Paris.
