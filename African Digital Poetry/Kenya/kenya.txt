Marjorie Oludhe MacGoye

Alternate names: Marjorie Phyllis King

Biography

Marjorie Phyllis Oludhe Macgoye was born 1928 in Southhampton, England. She wrote two poetry collections: Song of Nyarloka and Other Poems, a ballad about a young girl’s exploitation and Make It Sing and Other Poems, inspired by Stephen Mathenge, one of the military commanders of the fight against British colonialism. She went to Royal Holloway College, University of London for her undergrad in English. She also received a masters at Birkbeck, University of London in English. She moved to Kenya in 1954 to sell books and became a citizen in 1964. She died in 2015 in Nairobi, Kenya.

Jared Angira

Biography

Jared Angira was born in 1947 in Siaya, Kenya. Critically praised and recognized as a Marxist poet, Angira is the author of poetry collections such as Juices (1970), Silent Voices (1972), Cascades (1976), The Years Go By (1980), Soft Corals (1973). Tides of Time: Selected Poems (1996), and Lament of the Silent and Other Poems (2004). Angira attended the University of Nairobi, where he studied Commerce between 1968-1971. Aside being a poet, he also worked in the Kenyan Civil Service. He once said that Karl Marx is his teacher, while Pablo Neruda is his class prefect (in class) and his captain (on the battlefield).

Esther Nirina

Biography

Esther Nirina was born in 1932 on the central highlands of Madagascar. From 1953 to 1983, she lived and worked as a librarian at Orléans, France. She is the author of several poetry collections including Lente spirale (Spiral Lens) (1990), Multiple solitude (1997), Rien que lune : Oeuvres poétiques (Nothing but the Moon: Poetic Works) (1998), among others. Her second volume of poetry, Simple voyelle, was awarded the ADELF Grand Prix Littérature de Madagascar. In her writings, she focuses on themes of unity, creation, and after joining the group, "Les Amis de Rochefort," she learned about surrealism, which influenced her style of writing. She returned from France in 1990 and died in 2004 in Antananarivo, Madagascar.

Warsan Shire

Biography

Warsan Shire was born 1988 in Nairobi, Kenya. As a first-generation immigrant raised in London, her chapbooks, Teaching My Mother How To Give Birth (2011) and Her Blue Body (2015) explore the experiences of immigrants, refugees and other marginalized groups. In 2017, her work was featured in the Penguin Modern Poets series, Your Family, Your Body. She is the first ever Young Poetry Laureate of London and in 2014, was chosen as the poet-in-residence for Queensland, Australia. A recipient of the inaugural Brunel International African Poetry Prize in 2013, she has a Bachelor of Arts degree in Creative Writing from London Metropolitan University. Her full-length debut collection, Bless the Daughter Raised by a Voice in Her Head, is forthcoming in March 2022. Residing in Los Angeles now, she works as the poetry editor for SPOOK magazine.

Maina Kinyatti

Biography

Maina wa Kinyatti was born in 1944 in Kenya. He is most well known for his collections Thunder from the Mountains: Poems and Songs from the Mau Mau and A Season of Blood: Poems from Kenyan Prisons. He went to Farmington University for his undergrad. He also received a masters in History at the University of Michigan. In 1982, he was arrested during the Moi administration for possessing seditious literature and was imprisoned for six years. He subsequently fled to Tanzania before receiving asylum from the United States. He is the recipient of several awards, including PEN Freedom to Write Award.

Clifton Gachagua

Biography

Gagachua Clifton was born in 1987 in Nairobi. A poet and a novelist, he was awarded the inaugural Sillerman First Book Prize for African poets for his poetry collection titled Madman at Kilifi (2014). He attended Maseno University, where he studied Biotechnology. Gachagua’s work titled "The Cartographer of Water" was featured in a chapbook box set edited by Chris Abani and Kwame Dawes (Seven New Generation African Poets, Slapering Hol Press, USA). His novel, Zephyrion, was longlisted for Kwani? Manuscript Prize Project.

Ngwatilo Mawiyoo

Biography

Ngwatilo Mawiyoo was born in 1983 in Nairobi, Kenya. Her chapbook, Dagoretti Corner, was published in 2016 as part of the New Generation African Poets series. She is also the author of poetry collection, Blue mothertongue (2010). Writer, filmmaker and poet, she has been twice shortlisted for Brunel University’s African Poetry Prize, and twice nominated for the Pushcart Prize. In her work, she explores diverse rural Kenyan communities and its geohistories. Ngwatilo has a BA in Music from St. Lawrence University and in 2016, she received her MFA in Creative Writing from the University of British Columbia. She lives in Kenya.

Mukoma wa Ngugi

Biography

Mukoma Wa Ngugi was born in Evanston, Illinois in 1971, and grew up in Kenya. He holds a BA in English and Political Science from Albright College, an MA in Creative Writing from Boston University, and a PhD in English from University of Wisconsin, Madison. He is the author of the poetry collections, Hurling Words at Consciousness (2006) and Logotherapy (2016), as well as novels and critical books on African literature and politics. A prolific writer and academic, Mukoma Wa Ngugi was shortlisted for the Caine Prize for African Writing in 2009 and the Penguin Prize for African Writing in 2010. He is the co-founder of the Mabati-Cornell Kiswahili Prize for African Literature.
