Warsan Shire

Biography

Warsan Shire was born 1988 in Nairobi, Kenya. As a first-generation immigrant raised in London, her chapbooks, Teaching My Mother How To Give Birth (2011) and Her Blue Body (2015) explore the experiences of immigrants, refugees and other marginalized groups. In 2017, her work was featured in the Penguin Modern Poets series, Your Family, Your Body. She is the first ever Young Poetry Laureate of London and in 2014, was chosen as the poet-in-residence for Queensland, Australia. A recipient of the inaugural Brunel International African Poetry Prize in 2013, she has a Bachelor of Arts degree in Creative Writing from London Metropolitan University. Her full-length debut collection, Bless the Daughter Raised by a Voice in Her Head, is forthcoming in March 2022. Residing in Los Angeles now, she works as the poetry editor for SPOOK magazine.
