Marjorie Oludhe MacGoye

Alternate names: Marjorie Phyllis King

Biography

Marjorie Phyllis Oludhe Macgoye was born 1928 in Southhampton, England. She wrote two poetry collections: Song of Nyarloka and Other Poems, a ballad about a young girlâ€™s exploitation and Make It Sing and Other Poems, inspired by Stephen Mathenge, one of the military commanders of the fight against British colonialism. She went to Royal Holloway College, University of London for her undergrad in English. She also received a masters at Birkbeck, University of London in English. She moved to Kenya in 1954 to sell books and became a citizen in 1964. She died in 2015 in Nairobi, Kenya.
