Togara Muzanenhamo

Biography

Muzanenhamo Togara was born in 1975 in Lusaka, Zambia and spent his childhood in Zimbabwe. Poet and journalist, his first collection, Spirit Brides (2006) was shortlisted for the Jerwood Anderson First Collection Prize. His other poetry collections are Gumiguru (2014), which was also shortlisted for the Glenna Luschei Prize for African Poetry, Textures, winner of the National Arts and Merit Award for Literature, and Virga (2021). In 2001, he received his MA in Creative Writing from Manchester Metropolitan University. He also studied Business Administration in the Netherlands and in France.

Ndaba Sibanda

Biography

Ndaba Sibanda is a Bulawayo-born poet, novelist, and nonfiction writer. A prolific and critically-acclaimed writer, he is the author of The Gushungo Way, Sleeping Rivers, Love O’clock, The Dead Must Be Sobbing, Football of Fools, Cutting-edge Cache, Of the Saliva and the Tongue, When Inspiration Sings In Silence, The Way Forward, Poetry Pharmacy, Sometimes Seasons Come With Unseasonal Harvests, As If They Minded, and The Dangers Of Child Marriages: Billions Of Dollars Lost In Earnings And Human Capital. Ndaba has been nominated for National Arts Merit Awards, The Pushcart Prize and Best of the Net.

Ignatius T Mabasa

Alternate names: Tirivangani

Biography

Ignatius Mabasa was born 1971 in Mount Darwin, Zimbabwe. He has written two books of poetry in Shona—a Tipeiwo Dariro and Muchinokoro Kunaka— along with three novels and two children’s books. He studied Shona and Linguistics in University of Zimbabwe. He also received his masters and PhD at University of Oslo and Rhodes University. He went on to become a professor at University of Zimbabwe. He founded Bhabhu Books, a publishing company for Shona, Ndebele, and other vernacular language novels, stories novels and stories.

Chenjerai Hove

Biography

Chenjerai Hove was born February 9, 1956 in Zimbabwe. He wrote several books of poetry, novels and essay collections. His books of poetry include, Up in Arms, Red Hills of Home and Blind Moon. In 2001, he went into exile for his criticism of the Mugabe government. During his exile, he continued to write and was a fellow of the International Writers Project at Brown University's Watson Institute for International Studies. He was also Writer-in-Residence at the Florida Center for the Literary Arts at Miami Dade County College, and later a visiting Professor and Fellow at Brown University. He died in 2015 in Norway.
