Kojo Laing

Alternate names: Bernard

Biography

Kojo Laing was born 1946 in Kumasi, Ghana. One of Ghana’s leading poets and novelists, he is the author of the 1989 poetry collection Godhorse. His work has been praised for its linguistic originality where he takes a hybrid approach by blending English with Pidgin and vernacular language. Laing received his diploma in Management from the College of Management in Ghana before attending Birmingham University, England for his studies in rural management. In 1968, he graduated from Glasgow University with an MA in Political Science and History. Laing was the recipient of the National Poetry Prize Valco Award in 1976 and the 1985 National Novel Prize by the Ghana Association of Writers. He died in Ghana on 20 April 2017.
