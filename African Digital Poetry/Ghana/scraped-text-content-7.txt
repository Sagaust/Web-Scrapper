Kofi Anyidoho

Alternate names: born George Kofi Nyidevu Awoonor-Williams

Biography

Anyidoho Kofi was born in 1947 in Wheta, Ghana. A Professor of Literature at the University of Ghana, Anyidoho is the author of Elegy for the Revolution (1978), A Harvest of Our Dreams (1985), Earthchild, with Brain Surgery (1985), AncestralLogic and CaribbeanBlues (1992), Praise Song for the Land: Poems of Hope & Love & Care (2002), The Place We Call Home and Other Poems (2011). Anyidoho attended the University of Ghana, Legon; Indiana University, Bloomington, and University of Texas, Austin. An award winning poet, some of his prizes include the Langston Hughes Prize, the Davidson Nichol Prize Valco Fund Literary Award, the Fania Kruger Fellowship for Poetry of Social Vision, and BBC Arts and Africa Poetry Award.
