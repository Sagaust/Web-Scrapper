Abena Busia

Biography

Abena Busia was born 1953 in Accra, Ghana. Her published works include Testimonies of Exile (1990) and Traces of a Life: A Collection of Elegies and Praise Poems (2008). She went to St Anne's College in Oxford for her bachelors in English language and Literature and received her D.Phil. in Social Anthropology from St. Antony's College. As a scholar, her research focuses on gender, race, multi-cultural, and African diaspora studies and she is the co-director and co-editor of the Women Writing Africa Project, a multi-volume anthology presenting African Women’s voices and examining cultural and historical contexts of their writings. In 2017, she was appointed Ghana's ambassador to Brazil. She was a Professor of English at Rutgers University for forty years until her retirement in 2021.
