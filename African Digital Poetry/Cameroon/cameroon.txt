Alain Lawo-Sukam

Biography

Alain Lawo-Sukam was born in 1973 in Bangwa, Cameroon. Scholar and creative writer, he is the author of trilingual poetry book, Sueño con África/ Dream of Africa/ Rêve d'Afrique (2013). His work examines African experiences in the Hispanic Atlantic world. In 2005, he earned a Ph.D. in Spanish from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. He also received his B.Sc and MA from University of Yaounde I in 1995 and 1997 respectively. Lawo-Sukam is an Associate Professor of Hispanic and Africana Studies and the Coordinator of Africana Studies program at Texas A&M University.

Peter Vakunta

Biography

Peter Wuteh Vakunta is a native of Bamunka-Ndop in the Northwest Region of the Republic of Cameroon. He earned Bachelor's degrees in Cameroon and Nigeria, as well as MA and MS degrees in the US and Cameroon. He earned his Ph.D. in French and Francophone Studies with a minor in linguistics from the University of Wisconsin Madison. He is the author of poetry collections such as Bearing Witness (2020, Spears Media Press, Cameroon),  Straddling the Mungo: A Book of Poems in English and French (2009. Langaa RPCIG, Cameroon), Green Rape: Poetry for the Environment (2008. Langaa RPCIG, Cameroon), Poems from Abakwa in Cameroon Pidgin English (2015, Langaa RPCIG, Cameroon), Takembeng and other Poems from Abakwa (2015, Langaa RPCIG, Cameroon), Requiem pour Ongola en camfranglais: une poetique camerounaise (2015, Langaa RPCIG, Cameroon), Martryrdom and Other Freedom Poems (2010, Langaa RPCIG, Cameroon), and other collections. He is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Modern Languages in the United States of America.
