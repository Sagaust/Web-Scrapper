Vonani Bila

Biography

Vonani Bila was born in 1972 in Shirley Village, Limpopo, South Africa. Musician and poet, he is the author of several poetry collections including No Free Sleeping, co-authored with Alan Finlay and Donald Parenzee (1998), In the name of Amandla (2004) and Bilakhulu (2015). Director of the Timbila Poetry Project, he has been instrumental encouraging emerging writers and the works of marginalised poets. Bila was nominated for the DaimlerChrysler Award for Poetry in 2005. He went to Rhodes University for his Masters in Creative Writing and now teaches in the Department of English Studies at the University of Limpopo.

Nadine Botha

Biography

Nadine Botha was born in 1979 in Pietermaritzburg, South Africa. Poet, journalist and curator, she is the author of two poetry collections: Compared to Not Eating Tuna and Chocolate (2013) and Ants Moving The House Millimetres (2005). She went to Rhode University for her BA in Theory of Art and in 2015, she earned her MA in Design Curating and Writing from Design Academy Eindhoven. As a research designer, Botha’s work explores how political, scientific and cultural systems shape the designs in our in material reality. She lives in Rotterdam, Netherlands.

Dorian Haarhoff

Biography

Haarhoff Dorian was born 1944 in Kimberley, South Africa. He is the author of eight poetry collections, the most recent being ZenPenYen (2022). He edited The Inner Eye (1997), a poetry volume by students of the University of Namibia and the Windhoek College of Education. His writing is influenced by literary tradition, mythology, narrative therapy and ubuntu. He received his BA from Rhodes University (1965) and the University of South Africa (1972). He also holds a Master’s from Rand Afrikaans University (1983) and a D.Phil. from the University of York in England. A former Professor of English Literature at the University of Namibia, he facilitates creative writing workshops and serves as a mentor in writing residencies throughout South Africa.

Joan Hambidge

Alternate names: Helene

Biography

Joan Hambidge was born 1956 in Aliwal North, South Africa. Poet, reviewer and literary theorist, she is well known for her engagement with Afrikaans literary traditions. Her work covers a range of subjects including city life, love and family and ars poetica. Her book of poetry, Bitterlemoene (1986), was the recipient of the Eugène Marais Prize. She also received the ATKV Poetry Prize for her collection, Visums (2011). She earned a BA from Stellenbosch University and an MA from the University of Pretoria. She also holds two doctoral degrees, the first from Rhodes University and the second from the University of Cape Town. Hambidge is a professor at the School of Literature and Languages at the University of Cape Town.

Ingrid de Kok

Biography

Ingrid de Kok was born 1951 in Johannesburg, South Africa. She is most well known for her collections Familiar Ground, Transfer and Terrestrial Things, where she explores the possibilities of shaping the future by dealing with the past and considering the present.. She went to university of University of Witwatersrand for her undergrad in English and Political Science. She also received a Bachelor Honors with honors from the University of Cape Town and a masters at Queen’s University. She continued on to become an Associate Professor at the at the Centre for Extra-Mural Studies, University of Cape Town in South Africa.
