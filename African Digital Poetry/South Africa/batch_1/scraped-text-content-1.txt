Sheila Meiring Fugard

Biography

Sheila Meiring Fugard was born 1932 in Birmingham, England. At the age of 8, she relocated to South Africa where she studied as a drama student at the University of Cape Town. Poet and novelist, she’s the author of three poetry collections: Threshold (1975), Mystic Things (1981) and The Magic Scattering Of A Life (2006). After her marriage to playwright Athol Fugard in 1956, she was involved in acting and worked as a stage manager. Her first novel, The Castaways, won the Olive Schreiner Prize and CNA Literary Award for its striking exploration of South African history, themes of colonial conquest and the paranoia of apartheid and terrorism.
