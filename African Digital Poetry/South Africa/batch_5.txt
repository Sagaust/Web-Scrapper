Michael Cope

Biography

Michael Cope was born 1952 in Cape Town, South Africa. He has published several volumes of poems, including Scenes and Visions (1990), Birds of Different Feather (1992), Crossing the Desert (1993) and Ghaap, Sonnets from the Northern Cape (2005). In his writings, he explores the human origin, local cultures, and histories of South Africa. He is also known to feature “portrait sketches” of events and characters as well as employ the ballad form in his work. A novelist and artist jeweler, he studied at White River Primary School and SACS high school.

Geoffrey Haresnape

Biography

Geoffrey Haresnape was born in 1939 in Durban, South Africa. He has written several books of poetry, including Drive of the Tide, New: Born Images, Mulberries in Autumn, The Living and the Dead, and Where the Wind Wills. He is also the author of a novel, Testimony, and several plays based on his poetry. He is an emeritus professor of English and Creative Writing at the University of Cape Town, a life member of Clare Hall at the Cambridge University, vice president of the English Academy of Southern Africa, and an honorable member of PEN South Africa.

Ronelda Kamfer

Biography

Ronelda Kamfer was born in 1981 in South Africa. She has written four books of poetry, including Noudat Slapende Honde (Now Then Sleeping Dog), Santenkraam, Mammie and Chinatown. She typically writes in Kaaps-Afrikaans which contains English and South African slang, exploring complex subjects such as rape, humiliation, domestic violence, race, poverty and the position and role of women. She has a masters degree from the University of Western Cape in Afrikaans and Dutch. She is the recipient of the Eugene Marais prize from the South African Academy of Science and Arts, among other prizes.

Rustum Kozain

Biography

Rustum Kozain was born 1966 in Paarl, South Africa. He is most well known for his collections of poetry This Carting Life and Groundwork. He went to the University of Cape Town for his masters in English and Literature/ Letters. He also received his masters at Bowling Green University. He continued on to become a Professor at the University of Cape Town in South Africa. He has received numerous awards for his poetry, including the Nelson Mandela Poetry Prize, The Thomas Pringle Award and Ingrid Jonker Prize.

Alan Finlay

Biography

Alan Finlay is a writer, researcher and editor from Johannesburg. He is the author of poetry collections which include "Burning Aloes" (Dye Hard Press, 1994), "No Free Sleeping" with Donald Parenzee and Vonani Bila (Botsotso Publishing, 1998), "The Red Laughter of Guns in Green Summer Rain" – chainpoems with Philip Zhuwao (Dye Hard Press, 2002), and "pushing from the bank (Dye Bank, 2010). In 1994, with Robert Berold, he co-edited a collection of Eastern Cape school poetry called Parking Space (Institute for the Study of English in Africa). He has also co-edited a collection of new South African prose and poetry with Arja Salafranca called glass jars among trees (Jacana, 2003). Alan’s recent collection, pushing from the riverbank, was published by Dye Hard Press in 2010. He teaches communications for social change at the Wits University Journalism Programme.
