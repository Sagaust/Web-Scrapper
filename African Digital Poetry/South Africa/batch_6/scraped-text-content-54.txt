Dan Wylie

Biography

Dan Wylie was born in 1959 in Bulawayo, Zimbabwe. His poetry collection, The Road Out (1996), won the 1998 Ingrid Jonker and Olive Schreiner prizes. His other works include Road Work (2007), Slow Fires (2013), and Raven Games (2017). Poet, novelist and essayist, he concentrates on ecological issues in his writings, with further interests in the history of Shaka Zulu and Zimbabwean literature. In 1995, he earned his Ph.D. on white myths of Shaka from Rhodes University, Makhanda, South Africa, where he remains Professor Emeritus.
