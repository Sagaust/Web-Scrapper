Finuala Dowling

Biography

Finuala Dowling was born 1962 in Cape Town, South Africa. Her first poetry collection, I Flying (2002), was the recipient of the Ingrid Jonker Prize. Her other collections include winner of the Sanlam Award for Poetry, Doo-Wop Girls of the Universe, Penguin (2006), Notes from the Dementia Ward (2008) which won the Olive Schreiner Prize and Pretend You Don't Know Me (2008). She received an MA in English from the University of Cape Town (UCT) in 1986 and a D.Litt. from the University of South Africa (UNISA) in 1995. She was a Senior Lecturer at the Centre for Extra-Mural Studies, University of Cape Town from 2014-2021. Dowling is now a Freelance creative writing lecturer, and she also develops literary scripts which have been performed by John Maytham at a variety of venues in Western Cape.

Breyten Breytenbach

Biography

Breyten Breytenbach was born 1939 in Bonnievale in Eastern Cape, South Africa. He has published more than fifty books, including books of poetry, novels, journals and a memoir. Some of his poetry collections include The Iron Cow Must Sweat (Die ysterkoei moet sweet) (1964), In Africa even the flies are happy (1978), Nine Landscapes of our Times Bequeathed to a Beloved (1993). He went to university of Cape Town where he studied Philosophy and Painting. In 2017, he was selected as Laureate of the Zbigniew Herbert International Literary Award. Breytenbach is well known for his political activism, especially his active involvement in the anti-apartheid movement in the 1960s and 1970s. Following his increased radicalization, he was arrested, charged with terrorism, convicted, and in November 1975 received a nine-year sentence until he was summarily freed in December 1982 and he moved to Paris, where he lives with his wife.

Sarah Johnson

Biography

Sarah Johnson was born in 1980, South Africa. She is most well known for her collection Personae which assumes the identities of a variety of figures, exploring the intersection between the devotional and the erotic, among other things. She went to university of Cape Town for her undergraduate in English and Linguistics. She also received her MA in Creative Writing from University of the Cape Town. Personae was published in 2004 and described by accomplished South African poet, Stephen Watson as “one of the best debut volumes in recent years.”

Marlene Niekerk

Biography

Marlene van Niekerk was born in 1954 in Western Cape, South Africa. Her first poetry volume, Sprokkelster (1977), won the Eugène Marais Prize and the Ingrid Jonker Prize. Her other poetry collection, Kaar, won three national prizes: The Herzog prize, the Elisabeth Eybers Prize and the University of Johannesburg prize. Poet, novelist, critic and short story writer, she is known to examine post-apartheid South African society in her work. In 2011, she was awarded South Africa’s high honour, the Order of Ikhamanga, for her “outstanding intellectual contribution to literary arts and culture field through poetry, literature and philosophical works.” She is a Professor of Afrikaans and Dutch literature and Creative Writing at Stellenbosch University in South Africa.
