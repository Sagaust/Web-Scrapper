Chris Mann

Alternate names: Christopher Michael Zithulele

Biography

Chris Mann was born in Port Elizabeth, South Africa. Poet, playwright, and musician, he has published several collections of poetry including First Poems (1979) and A New Book of South African Verse (1979). His third volume, New Shades (1982) was the recipient of the Olive Schreiner Prize. In 2007, he received the Thomas Pringle Award for Poetry and in 2019, he was awarded the Patricia Schonstein Poetry in McGregor Prize. He attended the University of the Witwatersrand where he studied English and Philosophy. He also went to Oxford University where he earned his MA in English Language and Literature. He is the founder of Wordfest, developed as part of the National Arts Festival to celebrate languages and literature. Mann was the first Professor of Poetry at Rhodes University, where he lectured from 1977 to 1980. He died in 2021 in Makhanda, Eastern Cape.
