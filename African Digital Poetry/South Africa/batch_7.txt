Harry Garuba

Biography

Harry Garuba was born 1958 in Akure, Nigeria. At 24, he published his debut collection, Shadow and Dream and Other Poems (1982) which was first runner-up in the inaugural All-Africa Okigbo prize for poetry. In 1988, he received his MA and PhD from University of Ibadan, where he also served as an assistant lecturer in the English department from 1981 to 1997. Garuba was a member of the editorial advisory board of the Heinemann African Writers Series and was well known for his active interest in African and postcolonial literatures. In 1998, he moved to South Africa and joined the faculty at University of Cape Town in 2001. He died Cape Town at the age of 61.

Antjie Krog

Biography

Antjie Krog was born 1952 in Freestate, South Africa. She has written numerous books of poetry in Afrikaans, including Dogter van Jefta, Januarie-suite, and Beminde Antarktika and several of her collections have been translated into English. She went to the University of the Orange Freestate for her undergrad in Afrikaans, Philosophy and English. She also received a masters in Afrikaans at the University of Pretoria and a Teachers diploma at the University of South Africa. She went on to become a journalist and academic and has received numerous awards for her literary work and journalism.

Chris Mann

Alternate names: Christopher Michael Zithulele

Biography

Chris Mann was born in Port Elizabeth, South Africa. Poet, playwright, and musician, he has published several collections of poetry including First Poems (1979) and A New Book of South African Verse (1979). His third volume, New Shades (1982) was the recipient of the Olive Schreiner Prize. In 2007, he received the Thomas Pringle Award for Poetry and in 2019, he was awarded the Patricia Schonstein Poetry in McGregor Prize. He attended the University of the Witwatersrand where he studied English and Philosophy. He also went to Oxford University where he earned his MA in English Language and Literature. He is the founder of Wordfest, developed as part of the National Arts Festival to celebrate languages and literature. Mann was the first Professor of Poetry at Rhodes University, where he lectured from 1977 to 1980. He died in 2021 in Makhanda, Eastern Cape.

Allan Kolski Horwitz

Biography

Allan Kolski Horwitz was born in Vryburg, South Africa. He attended the University of Cape Town where he studied Philosophy and Literature. He is the author of four books of poetry: Call from the Free State, Saving Water, There are Two Birds at My Window and The Colours of Our Flag. He has also written and directed several plays, including The Pump Room, Comrade Babble, Boykie and Girlie, Jerico, Book Marks and Taking Everything into Account. He is a member of the Botsotso Jesters poetry performance group and of the Botsotso Publishing editorial board.
