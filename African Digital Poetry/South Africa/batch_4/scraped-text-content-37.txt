Makhaosazana Xaba

Biography

Makhosazana Xaba was born in 1957 in Greytown, KwaZulu-Natal, South Africa. She is the author of These Hands (2005), Tongues of Their Mothers (2008) and The Alkalinity of Bottled Water (2019). Poet, essayist and short story writer, her work is infused with activism as she explores the political and social challenges of her country’s present and past. Trained as a midwife and psychiatric nurse, she has served and contributed nationally and globally to areas of women’s rights and gender. During the second state of emergency in 1986, she went into exile and returned to South Africa in 1990 with the African National Congress Women’s League. She edited the poetry anthology “Like the untouchable wind: an anthology of poems” (2016) and “Our Word, Our Worlds: Writing on Black South African Women Poets” (2020). Xaba earned her MA degree in creative writing from University of the Witwatersrand, Johannesburg.
